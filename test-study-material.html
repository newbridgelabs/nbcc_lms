NBCC Church Membership Study Material

Section 1: Introduction to Church Membership

Welcome to the New Birth Christian Center (NBCC) membership study program. This comprehensive study will guide you through the fundamental principles of church membership and help you understand what it means to be part of our church family.

Church membership is more than just having your name on a list. It represents a commitment to:

  • Regular worship and fellowship
  • Spiritual growth and discipleship
  • Service to God and others
  • Financial stewardship
  • Unity and accountability

Section 2: Biblical Foundation of Church Membership

The concept of church membership is deeply rooted in Scripture. Throughout the New Testament, we see examples of believers joining together in organized communities of faith.

Key biblical passages include:

  • Acts 2:41-47 - The early church community
  • 1 Corinthians 12:12-27 - The body of Christ
  • Ephesians 4:11-16 - Building up the body
  • Hebrews 10:24-25 - Not forsaking the assembly

Section 3: Church Covenant and Commitment

When you become a member of NBCC, you enter into a covenant relationship with God and with your fellow believers. This covenant includes mutual responsibilities and commitments.

Our church covenant includes:

  • Commitment to regular attendance
  • Participation in church ministries
  • Financial support through tithes and offerings
  • Living according to biblical principles
  • Supporting church leadership

Section 4: Church Governance and Leadership

Understanding how our church is organized and led is important for every member. NBCC operates under a biblical model of church governance.

Our leadership structure includes:

  • Senior Pastor - Spiritual oversight and vision
  • Associate Pastors - Specialized ministry areas
  • Deacons - Practical service and support
  • Ministry Leaders - Specific program leadership

Section 5: Spiritual Growth and Discipleship

As a member of NBCC, you are expected to pursue spiritual growth and participate in discipleship opportunities.

Growth opportunities include:

  • Regular Bible study
  • Prayer and worship
  • Small group participation
  • Mentoring relationships
  • Service opportunities

Remember, spiritual growth is a lifelong journey, and we are here to support you every step of the way.
